How It's Calculated

The core future value of your investment is calculated using the formula for the future value of a series of payments (annuity), adapted for deposits at the beginning or end of the period:

$$FV = P(1 + r/n)^{nt} + PMT \times \left[ \frac{((1 + r/n)^{nt} - 1)}{(r/n)} \times (1 + r/n)^{\text{if beginning}} \right]$$

FV: Future Value of the investment

P: Initial principal balance

r: Annual interest rate

n: Number of times that interest is compounded per year

t: Number of years the money is invested for

PMT: Periodic payment

if beginning: This factor $(1 + r/n)$ is applied if deposits are made at the beginning of the period.

When solving for a missing variable (like time or interest rate), the calculator uses numerical methods (e.g., binary search) to find the value that satisfies the above equation for your target balance.
